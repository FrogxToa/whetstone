Gem Cultivation & Pet Competition

How both W6 ranking events work, what scores points, which tasks unlock the shop, and what the Monster Battle minigame requires.

Source In-game UI capture Compiled May 2026Filed 2026

Week 6 runs three simultaneous cultivation events: the Gem Cultivation Ranking, the Pet Competition (Ghost Pirate featured), and the Monster Battle minigame. All three share a 7-day window. The gem and pet events feed into separate ranking leaderboards; Monster Battle has its own scoring system. This guide covers all three.

01Gem Cultivation scores from your equipped gem overall rating. Continuously upgrading gems during the week raises your score.
02Pet Competition scores from acquiring UR pets (600 pts) and UR fragments (5 pts each). Synthesised pets do not count.
03Monster Battle entries require Happy Hammer Vouchers from the Daily Active Treasure Box. Full frenzy meter = Crazy Mode (2× points).
04Complete all Gem Cultivation tasks first — each one unlocks shop access and rewards a mystery box + 50 red diamonds.
§ 01

Gem Cultivation Ranking

7 days · Score = overall gem rating activated during event

How scoring works

Your score is your overall rating of gems that you have activated (equipped) during the event window. This is a cumulative metric — upgrading from a Lv.3 gem to a Lv.4 gem raises your overall rating and your score increases accordingly. Ties between players are broken by battle power.

Practical implication: embed lower-level advanced gems first (to tick the task requirements), then upgrade them progressively throughout the 7 days to keep climbing the ranking.

Shop access is gated by tasks The event shop only opens after you complete the related task. Finishing all tasks is the priority — do them in order from lowest gem level to highest, and knock out the two free social tasks immediately.

§ 02

Pet Competition (Ghost Pirate week)

7 days · Featured pet: Ghost Pirate (= JP Viking) · Score = overall pet cultivation rating

How scoring works

Your score is your overall rating of pet cultivation accumulated during the event window. The scoring is determined by two actions:

ActionPointsNotes
Obtain 1 UR Pet (directly) 600 Counts whether obtained via summon or any direct acquisition method.
Obtain 1 UR Pet Fragment 5 Every fragment of the featured UR pet earns 5 points.
Synthesise a pet from fragments 0 Pets synthesised from fragments are excluded from the score.
CriticalDo not synthesise the featured UR pet during the event. Synthesising converts your fragment pile (worth 5 pts each) into a synthesised pet worth 0 points. Accumulate fragments throughout the event for maximum score. Synthesise only after the event ends if you want to.

Ties between players are broken by battle power. The featured pets for this competition are UR class only — this week's featured pet is the Ghost Pirate (confirmed equivalent to the JP server's "Viking").

§ 03

Monster Battle

7 days · Entry cost: 1 Happy Hammer Voucher per run

How to enter

Each Monster Battle run consumes 1 Happy Hammer Voucher. These are obtained from the Active Treasure Box in Daily Task — meaning voucher supply is gated to your daily activity completion. Stockpile them throughout the week; do not spend packs frivolously before you understand your voucher capacity.

Gameplay mechanics

MechanicEffect
Bonus-point monsters Hitting them earns points and fills the frenzy energy meter.
Innocent monsters Hitting them deducts points. Avoid deliberately.
Frenzy Energy → Crazy Mode When the frenzy meter is full, Crazy Mode activates: monsters move faster and all point values double. Prioritise hitting bonus-point monsters during Crazy Mode for maximum score.
Battle Level Participating earns battle experience. Reaching a level threshold raises your combat level, granting an event impact bonus. Combat level resets when the event ends.

Re-claim mechanic (last day)

On the final day of the event, a re-claim ticket window opens. Two recovery options:

OptionWhen it appliesCost
Re-claim unused tickets You have unused Happy Hammer Vouchers at the end of the event Red diamonds (amount not captured)
Make up Reward Difference Value After each settlement, your rating is below your best recorded rating Red diamonds to bridge the gap between current and best rating
Efficiency note The re-claim system is a red diamond spend to recover value from unused vouchers. F2P players should focus on using vouchers regularly throughout the event rather than relying on last-day recovery, which adds cost. Red diamonds are better used elsewhere if you can maintain consistent daily play.
