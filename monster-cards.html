Monster Cards

Card compositions, T1 vs T2 replacement threshold, Soul of Attack/Guard/Defense levelling costs and caps, set bonuses, and category selector mechanics.

Source Elian (Top 50), Firefighter Ulam, EvilHelen · Discord #monster-cards Credits @the cutest Heet (cap data) Compiled Sep–Oct 2023Filed 2026

Monster cards are the most detailed sub-system in the game's character progression. ATK, Guard, and Def cards each have enhancement thresholds that unlock hidden stats. Tier 1 cards become available later and eventually outperform Tier 2 — but only past a specific total level threshold. The Soul of Attack/Guard/Defense system adds further depth on top.

CaveatSep–Oct 2023 data. The spreadsheet in the server (#bible, Monster Card sheet) provides live calculation; use it to confirm which card composition wins at your exact level.

§ 01

Card compositions

ATK slot options

8× Seruf

Pure Seruf ATK composition. Maximises Seruf's ATK contribution at the cost of Dyle's card-level scaling.

5× Seruf + 3× Dyle

Split composition. Commonly used compromise between ATK power and card-level scaling.

8× Dyle

Pure Dyle ATK composition. Scales with total card level. Recall Dyle scales off MC total level, not character level.

No forced combinationWork with what the system gives you. The same flexibility applies to Def and Guard card slots. Note: reaching the point where Dyle/Robo/Darkgoo outperforms by character level requires reaching a grand total of 500 card level — a significant investment.
§ 02

T1 vs T2 replacement threshold

500 total MC level

Do not replace T2 with T1 before 500 total card level

T1 cards give a fixed value of 20% for a 3-piece set. Below 500 total MC level, this does not outperform a well-levelled T2 set. Once you cross 500, the optimal composition shifts to 5× T1 + Dyle or Seruf (whichever you have more of) for ATK slots.

Use the Monster Card sheet in the server spreadsheet to confirm exactly at which level the crossover happens for your build — it may be slightly earlier or later than 500 depending on specific card levels. Ulam recommends rechecking every 5 character levels.

500
Minimum total MC level to start replacing T2 with T1T1 cards enter the ANE Dust shop gradually. Wait for this threshold before committing to a swap.
§ 03

Soul of Attack / Guard / Defense levelling

Extraction costs · Levels documented to 141+

Each Soul requires consuming cards from its own category. 12 purple cards of the category unlocks the first Soul. The effect: example Soul of Attack = +6.5% ATK for the first 10 seconds of battle. Levels 1–14 use purple and yellow cards; levels 15–60 introduce orange cards; levels 61–140 increase the orange requirement; level 141+ appears to reintroduce purple cards.

Keep 13 purple cards minimum

Each Soul requires exactly 12 purples from its category. Keep at least 13 in case of errors or future changes. Do not spend purples on other purposes if you have not yet extracted the Soul for that category.

Balance rule

From level 15 onwards, the three Souls (Attack, Guard, Defense) may not differ by more than 5 levels. Falling behind on one category prevents further extraction on the others. The level 50 cap documented in 2023 appears to have been extended by subsequent patches — the JP server confirmed costs beyond level 140.

§ 04

Set bonuses

Translation: Ulam · Credits: EvilHelen
2-set

HP boost

Increase HP of all cards in the category by 80%.

4-set

Character level scaling

For every 5 character levels, increase HP/ATK/DEF of all cards in the category by 2%, up to a maximum of 92%.

6-set

Monster card level scaling

For every 20 monster card levels, increase HP/ATK/DEF of all cards in the category by 2%, up to a maximum of 144%.

8-set

Soul level scaling

For each Soul of [Category] level, increase HP/ATK/DEF of all cards in the category by 2.5%, up to a maximum of 250%. The 8-set ceiling is the largest multiplier in the system.

Implication for Soul investmentThe 8-set bonus scales at 2.5% per Soul level up to 250%. Based on 2023 data, a level 50 Soul contributed 125% of that cap. If the cap has since been extended (as JP server data suggests), the ceiling may be reachable at a higher Soul level — the compound return logic holds regardless of where the actual cap sits.
§ 05

Levelling priority and card quality

Orange vs yellow · ATK first
  1. Priority order: ATK > Guard > Defense. Guard beats Defense because Guard's base stat is ATK%, while Defense's base stat is HP%. This also applies to the hit bonus unlocks documented in the Dojo Tips dossier (D-11).
  2. Orange cards over yellow whenever possible. Orange naturally provides more base bonus per 5 levels than yellow. Always embed orange cards as the priority.
  3. Exception for low-level orange cards. If your orange card will only reach level 10 at best, a yellow card with a set bonus may have an edge. Calculate before swapping — do not automatically replace yellow with orange if the orange is too low-level to realise its advantage.
§ 06

Category selector mechanics

Pity carries over · shared pool

Pity transfers when you change category

When you change the category on the Monster Card Categorised Summoning banner, your existing pity count carries over to the new category. You do not lose progress by switching. Banners for each category share the pity pool.

Category selector unlocks progressively

Categorised Summoning is locked until you meet the stated total card level requirement. Once unlocked, you choose a category — and summoning within that category applies to the shared pity counter. New categories open as server day count increases.

Licenses

Certain card features require a license purchase. The displayed expiry is misleading — licenses can be repurchased from the Past License tab even after the active window closes.

§ 07

Rainbow / Crimson Card system

New tier · Confirmed January 2026
Source noteThe following is sourced from the Japanese server Discord (Jan–Apr 2026). The JP server launched late 2024 and has a comparable content timeline to SEA. Mechanic names may differ from the SEA localisation. Verify against the in-game system before investing.

The Rainbow Card (also called Crimson Card in some translations) introduces a new tier above orange cards. It is a card enchantment system layered on top of the existing Monster Card structure, not a replacement for it.

Tier hierarchy

Red → Yellow → Orange → Rainbow/Crimson. Rainbow is the current ceiling tier. It sits above orange and requires a separate enchantment process distinct from standard card levelling.

Set effects require 2+ same-tier cards

Set bonuses from Rainbow cards only activate when you have two or more Rainbow-tier cards of the same type equipped. A single Rainbow card does not trigger the set effect alone.

Reset mechanics

Resetting a Rainbow card reverts it to red tier — not orange. The enchantment materials used are returned unenchanted. Crystal and coin costs from the Rainbow process are refunded on reset.

Cost reference (Lv80)

Reaching level 80 in the Rainbow system costs approximately 826,600 magic crystals as reported on the JP server. Treat this figure as directional; exact SEA costs may vary by patch.

Investment caveatThe reset-to-red behaviour means failed Rainbow attempts are expensive to recover from. Do not commit to this system until you have confirmed the current SEA patch has introduced it and have verified the reset cost against in-game tooltips.
§ 08

Barter system

Card exchange · conversion rates

The Barter tab in the Monster Card menu lets you exchange cards you have in excess for cards you need. Exchange rates are not 1:1 — understand the conversion tiers before using it.

Use barter to resolve imbalances

If you have a surplus of one card type (e.g. too many ATK cards, not enough Guard cards to maintain the balance rule), barter lets you redistribute without waiting for the summon banner. The rate determines how many excess cards you give up per card received.

Do not barter orange cards for yellow

Barter rates make orange-to-yellow trades an unfavourable conversion. Orange cards are the priority investment tier (§ 05). If you need yellow cards, obtain them from the standard summon pool rather than trading down from orange.

§ 09

Event card packs

Fragment selector · orange card acquisition

During certain events (typically unlocked at character level 164+), a card fragment selector pack appears in the event shop. This is one of the few reliable ways to target specific orange card fragments rather than relying on random summons.

Prioritise the fragment selector over general packs

The orange card fragment selector lets you choose which card type to reinforce — ATK, Guard, or Defense. If you have an identified weak slot in your current build, the selector pack is the most efficient use of event currency compared to generic card packs that give random types.
