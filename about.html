Sharper than a screenshot.

Whetstone exists because community knowledge in MapleStory R: Evolution lives in 2,000-message Discord threads, faded forum posts, and screenshots of screenshots. The intent here is to file the useful parts properly, date them honestly, and flag what is likely to have changed.

§ 01

House standards

Three principles
I.

Skeptical first

Every dossier flags the claims it cannot independently verify. Numbers from secondhand sources are marked as such. Absolute statements like "never touch this" are treated as defaults to confirm in-game, not eternal truths.

II.

Sourced, not invented

Where a guide draws on community advice, the original player, post date, and channel are cited in the article metadata. Where the original advice has aged, the dossier explains what is likely to have shifted since.

III.

Unofficial, on purpose

No affiliation with Nexon, the developer, or any sponsor. No paywalls. No referral codes. If that ever changes, the page will say so clearly. None do today.

§ 02

Editorial process

How a dossier gets filed
1
Collect Source material is gathered from in-game UI, Discord threads, community spreadsheets, and player notes. Screenshots and timestamps are preserved.
Phase 01
2
Audit shelf life Each claim is dated. Anything older than six months is reviewed against current patch notes. If it cannot be verified, it gets a visible caveat block, not a quiet trim.
Phase 02
3
Rewrite for the reader Discord prose is restructured into sections with TL;DR strips, priority ladders, and side-by-side comparisons. The point is to be scannable on a phone and complete on a desktop.
Phase 03
4
File and date Publication date, source dates, and revision dates all appear in article metadata. Outdated dossiers are marked, not deleted.

Colophon
