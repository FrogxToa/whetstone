Dossier No. 20

Dungeon System

Unlock levels, regular run rewards, main stage star drops, difficult mode equipment tables, and the skill manual tier progression across all ten dungeon tiers.

Source Cat ♦ Nancy · US server S004 · Google Sheets guide Server note US server — SEA names and values may differ Compiled May 2026Filed 2026

Dungeons are gated behind character level and unlock progressively as you push into new map zones. Each tier offers a regular run for daily resources and a difficult mode for targeted gear drops. The star material system (Star Souls, Star Sources, Star Force) is the primary reason to run dungeons consistently at every stage.

Source caveatThis dossier is compiled from Nancy's US server guide (S004). Dungeon names, unlock levels, and reward quantities may differ on the SEA server. Difficulty mode rewards for tiers VI–X were marked incomplete in the source — those rows are noted as unconfirmed. Verify against your in-game dungeon list before planning gear progression.

§ 02

Regular run rewards

Per-run baseline · Crazy mode gives +10% XP

Each dungeon tier gives Light Blue Ore, Gold Coins, XP, and Friend Points per run. Crazy mode increases XP by 10% (120k → 132k). Tier V regular rewards were not documented in the source.

§ 03

Main stage drops — stars and abyss

Star Souls · Star Sources · Star Force · Abyss Pass

The main stage of each dungeon drops the three star material types and Abyss Passes. Star Souls (red), Star Sources (yellow), and Star Force (purple) all increase in quantity at higher tiers. The Abyss Pass count is consistent at 30 per run across all documented tiers.

Star Soul breakpoint at Tier III

Star Soul yield doubles from 1 to 2 at Dungeon III (character level 128). If Star Souls are your bottleneck, pushing to Tier III is a meaningful progression step independent of gear reward considerations.

Star Force acceleration at Tier IV

Star Force jumps from 60 to 70 per run at Tier IV (level 147). A further jump to 80 occurs at Tier VII (level 185). Star Force is typically the most abundant of the three — prioritise Star Souls and Star Sources when optimising.

Equipment rarity progressionThe clear pattern across tiers I–V: Purple gear at Easy, Golden gear at Hard, Orange gear from Advance onward. Each tier also drops a secondary (lower level) version of the same gear slot. Expedition Badges scale with tier and are used in the Expedition Shop — the Tier V badge yield of 180 per run is more than triple the Tier I yield.
§ 05

Skill manual tiers

Rage Skill Breakthrough · 5 colours

Skill Manuals are used for Rage Skill Breakthrough. They are colour-coded by tier and drop from dungeon runs, with higher tiers unlocking the higher-colour manuals. Each manual corresponds to one Breakthrough level.

§ 06

Difficulty tier reference

Ten named tiers · I–V confirmed

The game uses named difficulty tiers rather than numbered ones. The sequence is fixed — each dungeon tier unlocks its corresponding named difficulty. Tiers I–V are confirmed in this dossier; VI–X are named but unconfirmed on reward details.

Confirmed tiers (I–V)

  1. Easy — Dungeon I (Henesys, Lv110)
  2. Hard — Dungeon II (Kerning Swamp, Lv117)
  3. Advance — Dungeon III (Gold Beach, Lv128)
  4. Nightmare — Dungeon IV (Perion, Lv147)
  5. Hell — Dungeon V (Orbis, Lv163)

Confirmed names only (VI–X)

  1. Shura — Dungeon VI (Verne Mines, Lv172)
  2. Chaos — Dungeon VII (Nihal Desert, Lv185)
  3. Despair — Dungeon VIII (Aqua Road, Lv193)
  4. Havoc — Dungeon IX (level unknown)
  5. Epic — Dungeon X (level unknown)
