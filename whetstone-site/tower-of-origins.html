Tower of Origins

A field guide to honing-point allocation, seasonal counters, resource priorities, and the difference between a useful debuff and a self-inflicted one.

Source Elian (Top 50) · Discord #tower-of-origins Compiled Sep–Oct 2023 Filed 2026 Read 10 min

Tower of Origins rewards the player who reads carefully. Most of the difficulty in the late stages is self-inflicted: points spent on the wrong debuff, currency sunk into the wrong fragment, a pet equipped against the wrong season. This dossier consolidates a Top-50 player's notes from September and October 2023 into a single reference, and flags the parts that have likely shifted since.

Caveat before you read The source advice is roughly two years old at filing and comes from one player in one guild Discord. Seasonal themes, shop rotations, and balance numbers rotate. Treat absolute statements ("never touch the theme bonus") as defaults to verify in your current season, not eternal law.

§ 01

What honing actually does

Concept

Honing points are spent to make the encounter harder in exchange for stage clears. The trade is either "the mobs become stronger" or "you become weaker." The goal is to pick debuffs that move the difficulty needle without crippling your ability to clear. Your soul orb levels, stars, and deployed-orb stats determine how many stages you can survive at any given honing tier.

60
Maximum stored runs Daily reset gives 10 runs. They accumulate up to a 60-run cap, so a stuck stage is not an emergency. Build power, return, clear, repeat.
§ 02

Honing priority ladder

Spend order

Spend points top-down. The earlier rungs hurt you the least; later rungs are situational and require reading each skill in-game.

Pick freely

Basic — Safe Picks

Mob shield recovery, slight ATK increase. Cost 1 point per level. These buff mobs but do not add true damage to you.

Use with care

Normal Honing

Potion Weakening at 20%+ is the workhorse. Monster HP, Character Weakness, and HP Loss Punishment all directly cripple your character. Cost 2 points per level.

Avoid by default

Basic — Theme Bonus

Per the source, this grants mobs 10% true damage against you. Skip unless you have defensive scaling that justifies it. Verify the tooltip in-game.

Skeptical note The "10% true damage" claim against Theme Bonus is one player's secondhand report. Confirm in-game before treating it as a hard rule. True damage bypasses defense, so if accurate, it scales worst as you progress.
§ 03

When you cannot clear the next stage

Standard operating procedure

Do not force the run

The temptation is to brute-force a clear with current power. The better protocol is to bank runs while building strength.

  1. Continue running 10 daily tasks for the soul currency.
  2. Let unused runs accumulate up to the 60-run cap.
  3. Invest accumulated soul into orb advancement, enchantment, and bloom unlocks until your power crosses the next stage's threshold.
  4. Return and burn the stored runs as a single push.
Verify before banking Confirm the 60-run cap and the 10-runs-per-day daily reset in your current client. These values can change with patches.
§ 04

Seasonal counters

Reference season: Sep–Oct 2023

The season theme inflicts environmental damage independent of mob stats. The source documents one specific pairing for that period. The structure of the lesson generalizes; the specific pet does not.

Threat 01

Gale Thunder

15% MAX HP true damage · 0.6s stun · knockback

Lightning bolts fall periodically. Stuns interrupt skill rotations, and the true damage scales with your max HP. Mobility and cooldown reduction help; raw defense does not.

Threat 02

Burning

10% MAX HP true damage tick · 10-stack burn · +1%/stack/sec

Firestones create burning areas. Burn stacks accumulate; at 10 stacks you take 10% MAX HP per second as true damage. Resistance and movement out of the burn area are the only real mitigations.

SSR

Counter pet: Stumpy +13

The source recommends Stumpy for burn-heavy seasons. Its Fountain of Life Lv.14 skill increases resistance to poisoning and burning by 41.0%. Confirm your Stumpy's level matches before assuming the same resistance figure.

Generalize, then verify Apply the principle: match pet skill to season DoT type. If the current season swaps Burning for Poison, Frost, or Bleed, the optimal pet changes.
§ 05

Resource allocation

P2W vs F2P

The source splits spending priority by progression archetype. The F2P path assumes you already own one Ephenia orb; if you do not, the order shifts.

P2W path

Assumes Balrog already maxed

  1. Evil Spirit Fragments — unlock additional Soul Blooms.
  2. Enchanting Stone — unlock total levels.
  3. Skip Balrog — already maxed at this tier.
  4. Save for new orbs — after bloom unlocks, accumulate for upcoming releases.

F2P path

Assumes 1× Ephenia owned

  1. Balrog Fragments — buy until Balrog reaches red base minimum.
  2. Save soul currency — wait for the next-generation orb release.
  3. Evil Spirit Fragments — later, once total star level prerequisites are met.
  4. Endgame target: total star level 10 → unlock Soul Bloom Lv.2 → max Balrog for optimization.
Reality check "Better orbs that release in the upcoming future" was the framing in September 2023. Whatever was "upcoming" then has likely launched. Check the current orb tier before saving indefinitely.
