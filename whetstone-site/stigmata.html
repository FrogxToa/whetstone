Stigmata Reference

Shard upgrade costs from activation through 5 stars, attack versus defense potential classifications, and notes on rage-affecting stigmata.

Source Elian (Top 50), Firefighter Ulam · Discord #stigmata-cultivation Compiled Sep–Oct 2023 Filed 2026 Read 3 min

A short reference channel. The most useful content is the shard cost table — knowing the running total from activation through each star level helps plan how many shards to accumulate before committing to an upgrade path. The potential classification is equally useful: most stigmata potentials are defense-based, and only two have confirmed attack-based potentials.

Caveat Shard costs and potential values are from September–October 2023. Verify current values in-game before planning a long shard accumulation. The "it will not show the next level skill unless you are at the last bar" UI note was accurate at the time — check whether this still applies in your current client version.

§ 02

Potential classifications

Attack vs defense · Source: Ulam

Most stigmata have defense-based potentials. Only two are confirmed as attack-based. This matters when choosing which stigmata to invest in for offensive builds.

Attack-based potentials

Two confirmed

Combat Scar 1% at Potential 3
Vampire Instinct 2% at Potential 3
Defense-based potentials

All others

The remaining stigmata potentials are defense-oriented. This is the default assumption for any stigmata not listed under attack-based above. If you are building for offense, Combat Scar and Vampire Instinct are the two worth targeting.

Rage cross-reference Vampire Instinct and Prom Mask both contribute to rage mechanics (see the Build Fundamentals dossier for the full rage reference). Vampire Instinct adds up to +2 initial self rage at +5 and reduces enemy initial rage by -1 at +3. Prom Mask adds +1 self initial rage at +3 and reduces enemy initial rage by up to -2 at +5. This makes Vampire Instinct a dual-purpose pick: attack-based potential AND rage contribution.
§ 03

3-star rage note

Closed Shop stigmata

Close Shop at 3 stars

The Close Shop stigmata unlocks a notable dual rage effect at 3 stars: +1 initial rage for the player and -1 PvP rage (reducing enemy initial rage in PvP). This is the only documented rage interaction for this specific stigmata in the channel.

Cross-referencing with the rage table in the Build Fundamentals dossier: the rage system has a self initial rage cap of +11 and an enemy initial rage reduction cap of -8. Close Shop contributes to both, making it a reasonable PvP investment if you are building toward those caps.
