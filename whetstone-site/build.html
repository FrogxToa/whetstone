Dossier No. 06

Build Fundamentals

Why hit rate outperforms raw ATK in Dojo, how to distribute monster cards, and a complete reference for rage mechanics across all pets, wings, and stigmata.

Source Elian (Top 50), Firefighter Ulam · Discord #build Rage credits @boom Compiled Sep–Oct 2023 Filed 2026 Read 6 min

Two questions come up constantly in build discussions: how to distribute monster cards, and which equipment affects rage. This dossier answers both with empirical data. The hit rate case study is a real before/after test run by a Top-50 player — the same pets, different card distributions, 20 more dojo floors cleared. The rage reference covers every known source of initial rage and rage gain across pets, wings, and stigmata.

Caveat before you read The case study reflects one player's build in September 2023. The rage reference table was compiled as of October 2023 and noted as including "upcoming" items at that time. Verify enhancement thresholds and rage values in your current client — these can shift with balance patches.

§ 01

Hit rate vs ATK: the case study

Empirical · Dojo

The conventional instinct is to stack ATK. The data below challenges that. By redistributing monster cards to favour hit rate over ATK, a Top-50 player cleared 20 additional Dojo floors — at the cost of 30,000 raw ATK. The same pets were used throughout, so the variable is exclusively the card distribution.

Before adjustment
Monster cards 25 / 20 / 10
Distribution ATK-heavy / Guard / Def
Hit rate 178.5%
Dojo cleared to Floor 483
→
After adjustment
Monster cards 20 / 20 / 20
Distribution ATK / Guard / Def (balanced)
Hit rate 220%
Dojo cleared to Floor 503

What was lost

The trade-off cost

-30k

raw ATK. A significant number on paper that mattered less than assumed once hit rate was deficient.

What was gained

The net result

+20

Dojo floors cleared. Hit rate at 178.5% was apparently below a threshold where missed hits negated the ATK advantage entirely.

Pets used in both tests

Held constant to isolate the card variable.

Seruf Eliza Viking Goby Android Skull Commander Dark Goo
Skeptical note This is a single data point from one player's build. The result may not generalise to all stat levels or content types. The principle — that insufficient hit rate undermines ATK — is mechanically sound, but the specific threshold where hit rate becomes the binding constraint will vary by character level, equipment tier, and the content being cleared.

§ 03

Rage mechanics reference

Initial rage · Rage gain · Credits: @boom

Rage determines how quickly you can use skills in combat. Two separate mechanics apply: initial rage (how much rage you start a fight with) and rage gain (how rage accumulates during combat). Both can be modified by pets, wings, and stigmata. The tables below cover all documented sources.

Build implications Green Wings and Flower Wings contribute to both self initial rage and enemy rage reduction simultaneously, making them efficient choices for PvP-focused builds. Prom Mask Stigmata is similarly dual-purpose. Seruf (up to +3 initial rage) and Eliza (rage preservation) form the core of a rage-optimised pet lineup and appear in Elian's own test lineup above.
