Dossier No. 08

Pet Compendium

All seven EXR pets with exact skills, optimal PvP and PvE lineups at two points in time, the bond system, EXR stats progression from +0 to +13, pull copy targets, and the UR swell table.

Source Elian (Top 50), Firefighter Ulam · Discord #pets EXR skills credits @Kim Stats credits @xRev, @Elian4245 Swell credits @the cutest Heet Period Sep–Dec 2023 Filed 2026

The pet system in MapleStory R evolves quickly. UR pets dominate early, then EXR pets start replacing specific slots as they release. The question is always the same: which slot does a new pet take, and is the copy investment worth it before the next banner? This dossier compiles the complete EXR skill reference, two lineup snapshots as the meta evolved, and the mechanical rules that govern pulling and progression.

Caveat before you read Two critical facts to internalize before anything else: EXR pets still use Moonstones — not a separate currency. And UR banners and EXR limited banners do not share pity count. Both were common points of confusion in the source channel. Lineups are snapshots from Sep 2023 and Dec 2023. The meta will have continued to shift; treat these as a baseline, not current truth.

§ 02

EXR compendium

All 7 EXR · skills · replacements

Release order 1–7. Skill descriptions are from Ulam's compiled list (Oct 2023), which is more precise than the original narrative posts.

EXR 01

Soul Teddy

PvP
Skill 2 Lower enemy ATK by 35%, reduce enemy petrify/stun/freeze resistance by 50% for 4 seconds. CD 6s.
Replaces in PvP: Skull Commander or Flying Fish. In PvE: Dyle (Dyle is defense-stat based; Soul Teddy's debuff is more useful).
EXR 02

Gatekeeper

PvP
Passive +30% HP shield at battle start. Recover 3% shield every 3 seconds. Shield cap 30%.
Replaces in PvP: Flying Fish (Flying Fish still usable in rage lock situations). Warriors: can replace Bone Fish instead.
EXR 03

Dark Cornian

PvE + PvP
Skill 4 100% chance to create a maple leaf zone for 3s. CD 8s. Grants +1 rage per second and +12% ATK while in zone.
Leap thresholds (zone duration): +4 = 1 rage/s for 2s · +9 = 1 rage/s for 3s
Note: Thief class has reduced synergy due to dash mechanic. PvE (non-rage-reduction bosses): replaces Eliza or Bone Fish. PvP: remove Eliza. Daily PvE: remove Dyle.
EXR 04

Hobgoblin

PvP
On Rage Skill Summon 2 ice totems. Each totem: freeze 3.4s, deal 134% ATK damage. CD 10s.
Replaces in PvP: Bone Fish — unless you want both stun (Bone Fish) and freeze (Hobgoblin). Otherwise swap any UR except Seruf or Viking.
EXR 05

Blue Dragon Turtle

PvP
Skill 3 400% ATK damage. Reduces target HP shield by 30% (cap: 100× ATK). Reduces self rage by 1. CD 6s.
Replaces: At this stage of the EXR lineup, swap out any other EXR or the weakest remaining UR.
EXR 06

Gryphon (Red Kentaurus)

Essential PvP
Passive Start with 6 barrier layers. Each layer: +10% DEF, +1% ATK. Nullifies 1 CC (stun/petrify/freeze) per layer. +1 layer every 20s, cap 6.
Replaces: Dyle — Gryphon's barrier layers make Dyle's resistance contribution redundant immediately.
EXR 07

Birk

Essential PvE
Passive + Rampage Activates at battle start. Every 20 seconds: +3 rage, +25% boss damage, auto skill deals +100% ATK as true damage for 10s.
Why this replaces Seruf/Viking: Viking's Skill 4 has a 10s CD — providing +2 rage per 20s. Birk provides +3 rage per 20s at the same interval, plus boss damage and true damage. Once Birk is at max leap, both Seruf and Viking become replaceable.
Replaces: Seruf and/or Viking — but only at Birk's maximum leap level.

§ 04

Bond system

Why +7 UR and +4 EXR matter

What bonds do

Bond stats are appended directly to the equipped pet's stats — automatically. They are not a separate passive; they stack onto the pet's attribute total. This makes UR bonds at +7 a meaningful stat multiplier rather than a cosmetic unlock.

Minimum EXR for bond activation

To activate the bonds on your +7 UR pets (e.g. Bone Fish, Seruf), you need to pull an EXR to at least +4. That requires 3 EXR copies. Pulling a single copy does not activate bonds — and cannot replace any slot — making a solo pull the worst possible use of Moonstones.

Should you pull EXR if you cannot reach +13? Yes — but target +4 at minimum. +4 activates bonds on your existing +7 UR pets. +6 unlocks Illustration. Only go all-in (toward +13) if the EXR is rated essential for your primary content (PvP or PvE). Budget for the next EXR before spending everything on the current one.

§ 06

Pull guidance and copy targets

EXR · Pity rules

Do not pull 1 copy

One copy cannot reach +1 enhancement and cannot replace any slot. It is a progression gatekeep. Commit to at least 3 copies (+4) or save entirely.

Copy targets

+4 = 3 copies — bonds activate on existing +7 UR pets. Minimum viable pull.

+6 = 4 copies — unlocks Illustration. Pull here if you can afford it without compromising the next EXR.

+13 = all-in — only for essential EXR.

Viking pull target

Viking is a key UR that remains in lineups until Birk at end-game.

F2P: +8 for the stats leap if possible, +6 as a safe minimum.
P2W: +9 minimum, +13 for full effect.

Pity rules

  1. EXR pity carries over between EXR banners. Confirmed in-game — pity count does not reset when a new EXR banner begins.
  2. UR banners and EXR limited banners are entirely separate pity pools. Pulls on a UR banner do not contribute to EXR pity, and vice versa.
  3. EXR Swell only becomes useful from +41 stars onwards. Do not sacrifice copies for Swell early. Save the copies for enhancement targets first.
