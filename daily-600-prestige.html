Daily 600 Prestige

How to hit 600 prestige for exactly 9 CD, which monthly packs a mid-spender should prioritize, and how to think about the legendary level benefits.

Source Elian (Top 50) · Discord #daily-600-prestige Compiled Sep 2023 Filed 2026 Read 4 min

Prestige points are earned by spending Coloured Diamonds on in-game packs. There is a daily target of 600 prestige that is worth hitting efficiently — the question is which combination of packs gets you there at the lowest CD cost. The answer is a specific three-pack combination totalling 9 CD. Beyond the daily target, this dossier also covers the monthly packs a mid-spender should consider and the blue diamond sinks that unlock at the legendary levels.

Caveat before you read All prices and pack contents are from September 2023. Pack availability, CD costs, and prestige values may have changed in subsequent updates. Verify current in-game values before spending.

§ 01

Hitting 600 prestige for 9 CD

Daily allocation

Three packs combine to hit exactly 600 prestige at a total cost of 9 CD. Each pack contributes differently — the Timer Maintenance Pack carries the most weight at 5 CD.

The primary pack. Highest individual CD cost but largest prestige contribution. Includes maintenance tickets as part of the daily allocation.

What accumulated prestige unlocks — Privilege track

Prestige from daily CD spending accumulates permanently and unlocks Privileges at milestone totals. Key milestones confirmed in-game: 1st top-up → Auto-Pathfinding + Auto-Loot Collection. 1,000 prestige → Quick Battle +1 purchase/day. 6,000 prestige → Quick Battle +1 (stacks). 20,000 prestige → Quick Battle +2 + Training Field purchase. 30,000 prestige → Summon Selection unlocked. Hitting 600/day means ~18 days to 10,800 prestige, ~33 days to 20,000.

Free daily tasks add 210 prestige

Completing all 12 daily tasks gives an additional 210 prestige/day at no CD cost. Combined with the 9 CD pack target (≈600), a player who completes all tasks nets ≈810 prestige on a full day. § 02

Mid-spender monthly essentials

Best value CD purchases

For a player who spends moderately — not P2W by any strict definition, but willing to invest in the highest-leverage recurring packs — these are the purchases with the strongest return relative to their CD cost.

Monthly Passes
~20 CD total · (6.98 + 20.98 SGD for both tiers)

Two-tier system. Grants 1 wish and 500 reds per day across the month.

Blessings stack up to 450 points every 30 days. The more you stack, the more UR fragments you can redeem. Do not skip days — the stacking value compounds.

Monthly Licence
20 CD

The headline reward comes at day 28: 270 UR Fragment Selector + dust + reds. That single day-28 claim is the reason this is considered non-negotiable for mid-spenders.

You do not need to purchase it immediately. Claims can be collected on the last day — plan the purchase timing around your prestige accumulation cycle.

Growth Fund 1
20 CD

One of two growth funds available. Combined value across both funds: 26,500 Blue Diamonds. Strong long-term value relative to the CD cost.

Like the licence, purchase timing can be deferred. Plan both growth funds together with your prestige cycle.

Growth Fund 2
20 CD

The second growth fund. Same mechanic as Fund 1. Buy both for the full 26,500 Blue Diamond value.

If budget is constrained, prioritize the Monthly Licence first (day-28 reward is time-sensitive within the licence period), then both growth funds.

80
Total CD for the full mid-spender stack Monthly Passes (20) + Monthly Licence (20) + Growth Fund 1 (20) + Growth Fund 2 (20) = 80 CD per month. At 1 CD = 1 USD, this is roughly $80 USD per month for the recommended recurring stack.
Timing note None of these need to be purchased on day one of the month. The key exception is the Monthly Licence — its day-28 reward is gated by how many days into the licence you are, so purchasing it late in a calendar period means waiting longer for the day-28 claim. Plan accordingly.
§ 03

Legendary level benefits

Blue Diamond sink

Two legendary level thresholds unlock UR pet selectors. Both are priced in Blue Diamonds rather than CDs — making them the largest Blue Diamond expenditure most players will encounter. The mechanic effectively asks you to convert Blue Diamonds into UR pets with reds as an additional return.

Should you buy?

The source's framing is direct: "if you need the UR it's really good." The mechanic converts Blue Diamonds — which you accumulate passively over time — into a UR pet plus reds and additional benefits. If the selector includes a UR you are actively building toward, the value is strong. If you are already covered on the available pets, the value drops considerably.

  1. Identify which UR pets you currently need or are working toward.
  2. Check whether that pet appears in the level 155 or level 168 selector.
  3. Accumulate Blue Diamonds toward 8,888 before the unlock — do not panic-spend blues on other items if one of these selectors is on your roadmap.
  4. At level 168, the selector expands to include Flying Fish. If Flying Fish is your target, wait for level 168 before spending.
Blue Diamond planning 8,888 Blue Diamonds is a substantial sink relative to the F2P income of roughly 136–200 Blue Diamonds per month (see the FAQs and Dailies dossier). At that rate, accumulating for one selector takes 44–65 months without spending. This is realistically a P2W or long-term milestone feature for F2P players.
