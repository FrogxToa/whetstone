Devour System

Unlock levels and server day requirements for each devour type, how the creature/devour mechanic works, and the two distinct devour categories for pets.

Source Elian (Top 50), Firefighter Ulam · Discord #devour-for-everything Compiled Sep–Oct 2023Filed 2026

Devour is the system for consuming surplus maxed copies of equipment and pets to gain permanent stats or creature EXP. Each devour category has its own level and server-day prerequisites. Understanding when each unlocks helps you plan which items to hold and which to feed in.

CaveatUnlock levels and server day requirements from Sep 2023. Verify in-game before holding items in anticipation of a devour unlock.
01Devour opens at level 160. All other devour types have additional server-day requirements.
02Only pets at +13 max advancement can have their duplicate copies devoured.
03Two pet devour pools: SSR/SR (creature EXP) and UR (skill bonuses).
04Soul orb devour unlocks at level 172, day 99 — the latest requirement of all types.
05Max a pet to +13 first. You cannot devour dupes until the base is maxed.

§ 02

How pet devour works

Creature system

The prerequisite

A pet must be at maximum advancement (+13) before any of its duplicate copies can be devoured. You cannot devour a +10 copy of a pet you have at +13 — the base pet must be fully maxed first.

This means devour planning is a late-stage activity. Build the base to +13, then accumulate surplus copies for feeding.

SSR / SR devour pool

Feeds into creature EXP. This is the general "sink" for extra SSR and SR pets once you have no further use for them as copies.

SSR: 50 EXP per copy SR: 10 EXP per copy

UR devour pool

Separate pool from SSR/SR. UR devour unlocks skill bonuses — shields, stuns, ATK and DEF bonuses — at later stages of the creature system. This is the higher-value pool and the primary reason to save +13 UR surplus copies.

Both pools are distinct SSR/SR EXP and UR skill bonuses do not mix. Feeding a UR into the SSR/SR pool (if even possible) would waste its higher potential value. Keep UR surpluses separate until you can confirm which pool accepts them.
§ 03

Stigmata devour

Unlock: Lv 150 · Day 78

Stigmata devour (Reconstruct) feeds surplus stigmata fragments for permanent stat gains. Orange stigmata give a higher stat return per piece than yellow. The before/after screenshots below show the gain from feeding a single orange stigmata.

Orange vs yellow

Feeding 30 yellow stigmata returns a documented value shown in the devour screen. Orange stigmata yield more per piece — save orange surplus for devour rather than converting to yellow first.

Do not feed before unlock

Stigmata devour requires level 150 and server day 78. If you are approaching that threshold, hold surplus stigmata rather than discarding them — they have devour value once the gate opens.

§ 04

Soul orb devour

Unlock: Lv 172 · Day 99 · Latest gate

Soul orb devour is the last devour type to unlock. Surplus soul orb fragments are fed for stat gains. Because this requires both level 172 and server day 99, it is unlikely to be accessible in the early server period — plan around it rather than waiting for it.

Hold orb fragments near the unlock threshold

Once you are within reach of level 172, stop spending soul orb fragments on minor upgrades if you have a surplus. The devour return may exceed the marginal gain from small incremental investments in lower-priority orbs.

§ 05

Mask tracker

Next unlock indicator

The Devour menu includes a Mask section with a top-right indicator showing the next unlockable mask. Masks are unlocked by reaching cumulative devour milestones — the tracker tells you how far you are from the next one.

Check the mask tracker before choosing what to devour

If you are close to a mask unlock threshold, prioritise devour actions that push you over it. Mask bonuses are permanent and apply across all content — the next unlock is often worth targeting deliberately rather than feeding items in arbitrarily.
