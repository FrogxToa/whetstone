Runes and Abyss

Rune skill priority, why solvents beat 5-star purchases, the Lucky Recast mechanic, and full Abyss tier requirements and rewards.

Source Elian (Top 50), Firefighter Ulam · Discord #runes-abyss Lucky Recast credits @Jisoo (S39) Compiled Sep–Oct 2023 Filed 2026 Read 7 min

Runes have more moving parts than they first appear — six slots, a separate unlock at level 140, an enhancement system, a recast system, and the Abyss as the primary currency source. Most early mistakes come from either enhancing the wrong skills or spending Abyss Energy on 5-star runes instead of solvents. This dossier covers both, plus the Lucky Recast mechanic and the full Abyss tier ladder.

Caveat before you read Content compiled September–October 2023. Abyss tier requirements, reward milestones, and recast costs may have changed since. Verify current values in-game before planning a grind.

§ 01

Rune basics

New player orientation

6 rune slots

The total number of runes in your primary loadout. Keep this in mind when collecting and enhancing — you need six to fill the page.

Level 140 unlock

An identical second rune page unlocks at level 140. Keep spare runes before hitting this level — you will want to fill the second page immediately.

Any quality works

C, B, and S runes can all be enhanced. Dismantling an enhanced rune returns every enhancement material. Enhance what you have — do not wait for S runes to start.

Cursed state

Certain rune skills apply a cursed debuff to targets. Cursed = increased critical damage taken. Some documented rune combinations apply cursed toxin for 3–4 seconds. Keep this in mind when evaluating offensive rune skill lines.

Rune Illustration passives by star tier

Each rune carries a hidden passive called the Illustration — a battle-start effect determined by its star tier. These are separate from the active skill lines you recast. Higher tiers unlock Arkarium-named conditional effects on top of the basic flat stat layers.

5★ Vortex Rune — Arkarium effects

Unlocks the full Arkarium passive set: Eternity (+Final DMG), Thunder (layered auto skill DMG, decays every 10s), Shadow (+ATK / drains 1% HP each 3s), Glimmer (cycling ATK buff+debuff), Ice and Fire (area DMG + burn), Curse (poison AoE). At Rune-I: 1.0–5.0% Final DMG / 1–2 Thunder layers (+7% auto DMG each) / 1.0–3.0% ATK.

4★ · 3★ · 2★

4★ Pink Bean: Advanced Hit Bonus (5–12%) + Basic DEF/ATK/EVD layers. 3★ Maple Leaf: Advanced DEF/ATK stacks (12–30 layers × 600 DEF or 2000 ATK each) + Advanced Hit Bonus. 2★: Basic DEF/ATK layers + Basic Crit Res/Crit Bonus. All tiers grant useful flat stats; only 5★ adds the Arkarium conditional effects.

Rune slot layout and stat contribution

Six rune slots form a ring around the central rune. The equipped rune's Basic Attrib (flat ATK/DEF) and Enhanced Attrib (resistances, EVD Bonus, etc.) feed directly into your character's Total Attribute Bonus. The Skill Entry line is the rune's active skill — evaluated separately in §02.

§ 02

Skill priority: keep vs skip

Rune skill evaluation

Not all rune skill lines are worth keeping. The following is a practical filter — skills too minor to justify holding a rune slot versus the skills worth building around.

✓

Keep these skills

  • Orange and yellow tier skills — colour tier correlates with power; these are the primary targets.
  • Cooldown runes — skill cooldown reduction is consistently valuable across all content.
  • Giant Blessings — notable power gain.
  • Braver with More Battle — combat performance skill worth retaining.
✕

Skip these skills

  • Movement Speed — too minor to affect outcomes.
  • +Ranks to Skill — marginal and not worth a slot.
  • Experienced: Hunting / Insight / Acuity — experience-type bonuses are low priority at this stage.
  • Mini Clan — negligible impact.
Reforging note If a rune has undesirable skills but good enhancement levels, you do not have to discard it. Skills can be reforged out and you will still recover the solvents used for enhancement after the reforge. Only recasting solvents are not returned on dismantle.
§ 03

Solvent economy: why solvents beat 5-star purchases

Abyss Energy allocation

The instinct is to spend Abyss Energy on 5-star (red) runes for the immediate rating boost. The math argues strongly against this. The solvent path to level 12 enhancement adds more rating per unit of energy spent than buying a red rune outright.

Buy 1 red rune with 10,000 Abyss Energy Immediate rating gain from a 5-star rune
~+30k rating
Spend 10,000 Abyss Energy on solvents instead ~19,800 solvents — enough to take approximately 4 runes to level 12
4 × level 12 runes
4,820
Solvents required per rune to reach level 12 enhancement At this cost, 19,800 solvents covers approximately 4 runes at level 12 — each contributing meaningfully to your rating, far outpacing the single red rune purchase.

What if you get a 5-star rune naturally?

If you pull a 5-star rune through normal play rather than purchasing, keep it. If it has good skills, reforge the skills in and you will recover the enhancement solvents. If you later find a better rune to replace it, dismantle — all enhancement solvents return. Recasting solvents do not return on dismantle, so be more conservative about recasting lower-quality runes.

Alternative: level 9 across five runes

If full level 12 on three runes feels too concentrated, an alternative is to bring five runes to level 9. This spreads the solvent investment more evenly, gains more rating per level at that range, and ensures you hit more attribute thresholds across the set.

Do not appraise yet Appraisal is not the right move until you have S-rank runes with 3 skill lines and your recast master is ready. Appraising before that point wastes resources on a rune you will likely replace. The source is explicit on this: "we are not yet at the stage that we are ready to appraise yet."
§ 04

Lucky Recast mechanic

Credits: @Jisoo (S39), compiled by Ulam

Lucky Recast is a pity mechanic built into the rune recast system. After a set number of recasts, it guarantees at least one S-rank attribute option in the next recast pull.

  • Recast your rune repeatedly. Each recast costs solvents (exact cost varies by rune tier — see Lucky Recast cost table in-game).
  • After 375 recasts, Lucky Recast is triggered automatically on the next recast.
  • Lucky Recast presents 4 random bonus attributes. One of the 4 is guaranteed to be S rank.
  • You may swap one of your existing bonus attributes with any of the 4 presented options — or decline to swap entirely.
  • Regardless of whether you choose to swap, Lucky Recast resets immediately after triggering. You need another 375 recasts to trigger it again.
Strategic implication If you are close to the 375 threshold and have a high-value S rank attribute in your current set that you do not want to risk replacing, you can still trigger Lucky Recast and simply decline the swap. The guaranteed S rank option is a floor, not a forced outcome. The pity counter always resets after triggering regardless of your choice.

Abyss Shop — Currency and Gameplay tabs

The Abyss Shop has two tabs. The Currency tab sells standard materials with Abyss gold: Magic Solvent (100g), Raise Runestone (400g), and mystery rune boxes by star tier (2★ = 1K, 3★ = 3K, 4★ = 6K, 5★ = 10K, limited to 3 each). The Gameplay tab sells special packs that unlock only after completing a set number of runs at a specific tier — these are separate from the standard currency purchases and reward 5★ rune mystery boxes.
